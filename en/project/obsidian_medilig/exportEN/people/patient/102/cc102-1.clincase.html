  Introduction
  The patient, 62 years old, former smoker, was admitted to the hospital with primary symptoms of difficulty breathing, chronic cough, and increased sputum production, which began worsening over the past six months.

  Initial Symptoms and Course
  The initial symptoms appeared approximately ten years ago with occasional shortness of breath and mild cough. The patient reported that the symptoms gradually worsened, especially in the mornings and during physical activity. Over the last six months, the shortness of breath became more severe and was accompanied by productive cough with yellow sputum.

  Risk Factors
  The patient has a history of smoking (20 years) and worked for many years in an environment with high levels of dust and chemical substances. There is no family history of pulmonary diseases.
